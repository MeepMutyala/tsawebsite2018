Our School's Programs

STEM Program-Pathways

Biotechnology: This 3 year major begins with an introductory unit in 9th grade integrated science on defining and exploring biotechnology. In subsequent years students will study Biomedical Technology, Food Science Biotechnology and Environmental Biotechnology. Students will have opportunities for action research and to participate in university research studies. At the end of this course of study, students will be prepared to pursue a college major which best fits their area of interest in biotechnology or a related field.

Computer Science: This 4 year major begins with foundational programming and application design. Students will begin working in HTML & Python, ultimately mastering Java while working towards the AP Computer Science Exam. Students will complete junior and senior level projects related to their specific area of interest in programming and applications.

Engineering: This 4 year major begins with foundations in solid modeling and engineering design. Over the course of study students will explore topics in biotechnical engineering, structural engineering and aerospace. The overall goal of this major is to become active problem solvers and collaborative team members in the process of researching and “engineering away” problems.

Computer Labs: The computer labs at MOT are designed for any projects that require technology. Coding and computer projects are done here.

Engineering Labs: The state of the art engineering labs at MOT includes 3D printers and engineering equipment that students can use to design, build, and create projects.

Science Labs: The state of the art science labs at MOT are designed for students to work on their science projects, conduct experiments, innovate, and invent.
TSA Advisor and Engineering Teacher Captain Seedorf

The engineering program at our school is managed by Captain Roger Seedorf. Captain Seedorf graduated from the United States Naval Academy with a Bachelor of Science in Engineering and Mathematics (Operations Analysis). Seedorf also possesses several Master degrees and certificates in Leadership and Management, Information Technology and Management, Nuclear Engineering, and Education. Captain Seedorf has completed some doctoral level work in Educational Technology. Over the last 14 years, Captain Seedorf has spent his time working as an instructor, mainly teaching technology applications, computer science, career development, and mathematics. Captain Seedorf was also the Cooperative Employment Coordinator and Career Placement Counselor in the New Castle County Vocational School District. Prior to his education career, Seedorf served as an officer in the United States Navy performing many different roles and functions, the highlight being the Captain of a nuclear submarine. In addition, he worked a few years in the industry as a Director of Operations and also spent a few years in a special government program as a Program Manager. Seedorf has coached various levels of sports for many years, including baseball, softball, field hockey, soccer, basketball, boxing and bowling.I have also coached for many years at various levels in baseball, softball, indoor field hockey, indoor soccer, boys and girls basketball, boxing, and bowling. Captain Seedorf is also the advisor of our TSA chapter, and has helped prepare our chapter to succeed at the TSA competition.
